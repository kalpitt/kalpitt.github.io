Saavdhan — An Offline Bodyguard for the Family Phone

It detects scam and spyware apps, explains the danger in plain Hindi or English, and walks the user to the exact fix screen — without ever touching the internet.

Saavdhan (सावधान, "be alert") is a free, open-source Android app built for non-technical Indian family members — the parents and grandparents targeted by WhatsApp-delivered banking trojans disguised as wedding invites, courier updates, and KYC notices. It is a detective and a guide, never an enforcer.

v0.6.0 Signed Release 100% Offline Hindi + English 131 Tests Green MIT Licensed

The Problem — The Scam Arrives on WhatsApp, Not in a Browser

India's most damaging phone scams no longer look like phishing emails. A family member receives an APK on WhatsApp — a "wedding invitation," an "electricity bill," a "courier update" — and one tap later a SpyNote-class banking trojan holds Accessibility and Device-Admin powers over the phone. The victims are precisely the people least equipped to diagnose it, and the existing tools fail them twice: security apps assume technical users, and most demand internet access and data collection — the very trust deficit that makes families hesitate to install anything at all.

Saavdhan's premise: for this audience, trust is the product. An anti-scam app earns adoption in a family WhatsApp group only if it can honestly say — and prove — that it collects nothing, sends nothing, and explains every verdict in the user's own language.

The Design — Three Promises, Engineered as Constraints

  • It can never touch the internet. The app declares no INTERNET permission, actively strips any that a dependency tries to merge in, and CI fails the build if the merged manifest ever contains it. The privacy promise is enforced by the build system, not by a policy page.
  • Every verdict is deterministic and explainable. A point-based risk engine in a pure Kotlin domain layer scores explicit signals — dangerous permission combinations, sideload delivery chains from messenger apps, impersonation of trusted brands (with fuzzy matching), missing trusted signatures. Every verdict carries its ranked reasons, so the UI can explain the danger in plain Hindi or English rather than issue a black-box score.
  • Detective, not enforcer — honest about OS walls. Android deliberately prevents one app from disabling another app's Accessibility or Device-Admin powers. Saavdhan never fakes an auto-fix: it deep-links the user to the exact settings screen, with OEM-aware fallback chains for Samsung, Xiaomi, Oppo, Vivo, and Huawei skins that degrade one honest step at a time.

What v0.6.0 Ships

  • One-tap scan of every installed app, with risk levels and plain-language reasons — bilingual throughout, with per-app language switching.
  • A guided cleanup checklist that walks the user, one honest step at a time, through removing a dangerous app's powers before uninstalling it.
  • A watchdog that alerts when a newly installed app looks dangerous — the moment that matters in a live scam.
  • Scam delivery-chain detection that flags apps sideloaded via messengers — the signature of the WhatsApp APK lure.
  • "Send result to family" — an offline share receipt so a son or daughter in another city can verify a parent's phone was scanned and what it found.
  • A signed, reproducible release on GitHub. Play Store submission is prepared but not yet filed.

Verified, With Honest Limits

  • 131 unit tests green across the risk engine and cleanup logic — testable precisely because the "brain" is a pure Kotlin domain layer with no Android imports.
  • CI enforces the promise: the build fails if the merged manifest gains an INTERNET permission; every user-facing string must exist in both English and Hindi.
  • Detection rules ship in-app and update only via app updates — a deliberate trade-off that keeps the zero-network promise absolute.
  • Some steps only a human can take. Android reserves certain switches for the user; Saavdhan says so plainly rather than pretending otherwise. Honesty about the wall is the feature.

Why It Matters

Saavdhan is a study in building for trust under constraints: a security product whose guarantees are enforced by the build system, whose judgments are deterministic and explainable in the user's own language, and whose honesty about what it cannot do is treated as a feature. It is the same engineering thesis as my flagship work — bounded, auditable judgment over black-box confidence — applied to protecting the least technical users from the most aggressive threats.
